Do you have a morning meeting (or “huddle”) each day before patients are seen?
I thought I would share some details about our current morning meeting agenda. I used to run the entire meeting each day, but a few years back I changed it up to give others some responsibility.
Our workday starts at 7am. We start seeing patients at 7:40 am. From 7am to 7:15am, staff begin setting up trays, re-stocking chairside, checking voicemails, etc.
At 7:15 (on the dot!) we gather in our New Patient Consult room (we have 12-14 staff).
I usually start with “happy Monday all” or something of the sort, and then the following team members begin.
Treatment Coordinator: TC begins the official meeting by going through all starts, if they need any Records taken, any medical or behavior issues, financial arrangements not completed, etc.
Lab Tech: announces any Debonds taking place, and what time Retainer Delivery is scheduled for. Also any other appliance delivers, or scheduled scans are noted.
Scheduling Coordinator 1: Discusses any same day or next day openings for full bondings, and exams. Then he/she discusses who is out today, lunches scheduled for the week, upcoming out of office days, etc.
Scheduling Coordinator 2: Mentions any Emergency Appointments on today’s schedule and the nature of each.
At this point, I go through all starts and assign a specific team member to each one. Finally, I end each morning meeting with 3 current core values. We used to recite our Mission Statement each morning but after years of this I felt we were just going through the motions so I decided to change it up!
Entire meeting lasts only 5-7 minutes but I feel it’s an invaluable start to our day!
